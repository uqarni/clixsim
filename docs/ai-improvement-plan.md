# Clix Engine AI Improvement Plan — Synthesis of 6 Analyst Reports

## Diagnosis in one paragraph

The human's 100% win rate is not one bug — it is a consistent playbook (screen every attack to ≥83% odds, kite/step-back, focus-fire arrivals, heal/resurrect everything back, pin-then-rear-flank, push only deep dials) running against an AI whose losses concentrate in **five structural holes**, each confirmed independently by 2+ analysts: (1) **movement scoring is threat-blind** — `_move_value` has zero terms for incoming fire, so the AI donated ~20 clicks per game walking into 92–97% kill zones (human hit rate 0.93–0.95 avg across all 3 games vs AI 0.43–0.77); (2) **the AI has no working formation path** — 1 LLM formation move vs 23 human across three games, and two analysts *geometrically verified* that one human blocking polygon silently deletes the candidate; (3) **target/push valuation ignores the two cliffs that decide games** — Demoralized clicks (42% of the Wings army's lost clicks were self-inflicted; the game literally ended on a self-push) and Necromancy/healing refunds (55% of Wings damage nullified; the Anvil healer was attacked 0 times because approach candidates only cover the 3 nearest enemies); (4) **the pre-game phases lose before turn 1** — a 111/200-point draft, "ranged formation" plans the engine forbids (Mage Spawn), zero healers in 3/3 drafts, and terrain candidates scanned from the *human's* edge with "near your side" mislabeled, gifting 3 hills per game; (5) **exploitable determinism** — the auto-face-the-mover free spin was farmed for rear kills in 3 of 5 Phalanx eliminations (human rear attacks 16 vs AI 2 lifetime). Verdict shared by all analysts: fix candidates + scoring + pre-game first; the LLM picker mostly rubber-stamps whatever the engine offers and ranks.

## Tier 1 — Do first (biggest win-rate delta per effort)

### 1.0 Eval harness: deterministic A/B self-play + archive counterfactuals *(enabler — build first)*
- **What**: Without this, none of the fixes below can be validated. The engine is deterministic and copyable, the heuristic is seed-reproducible, and the archives are full event logs — analyst 3 already demonstrated the key technique (rebuilt turn-1 Phalanx state from the archive, called `generate_formation_candidates`, proved the formation vanishes with the human's wall and appears without it).
- **Where**: new `clixengine/eval/` (or `tools/eval_harness.py`); reuses `Engine`, `HeuristicAI`, archive JSON loaders.
- **How**: three modes. (a) **Archive counterfactual replay**: reconstruct archived states, re-run candidate generation/scoring with a patch, count deltas ("formation candidates restored on N of 60 turns", "healer-approach offered by turn X"). (b) **Heuristic-vs-heuristic self-play**: N seeded games, baseline vs patched branch, fixed or pool-sampled drafts. (c) **Scripted "human playbook" bot** encoding the miner's six pillars (kite-back each turn, focus-fire wounded, pin-then-flank, castle shooters behind a healer) — necessary because heuristic-vs-heuristic won't exercise the exploits the human uses. **Metrics** (all computable from event logs today): win rate, net points differential, clicks dealt/received, % self-inflicted clicks (baseline: 42% Wings), rear-attack rate (baseline 2 vs 16), formation moves/game (1 vs 23), avg attack odds (0.43–0.77 vs 0.93–0.95), attacks per action slot (9/60 in Anvil), passes (28 vs 0), turns-to-first-contact-with-support.
- **Measure**: itself — run baseline vs baseline for variance calibration (~50 seeded games per arm).
- **Effort**: medium. **Evidence strength**: high (technique proven).

### 1.1 Formation-move robustness (3 analysts, verified twice)
- **What**: `_make_formation_move` tries exactly ONE rigid translation toward the single nearest enemy and returns `None` on any blockage; `_manual_formation_candidate` is explicitly withheld from the AI. Result: the AI's only tempo tool (3–5 figures per action vs 2 actions/turn) is disabled by a single human terrain piece. Anvil: formation dissolved t5, never re-formed (verified: translation toward Magus-behind-wall fails, toward the Artillerists succeeds). Phalanx: 5-figure Black Powder Rebels formation provably available at t1 iff the human's one custom polygon is removed — the AI advanced 2 figures/turn vs 4/turn and lost 102/111 pts to 0.
- **Where**: `clixengine/candidates.py:547 _make_formation_move`, generation loop at :705.
- **How**: (a) iterate 2–3 nearest enemies as translation targets; (b) on blockage probe rotated bearings (±30°/±60°) and shorter step fractions, exactly as `_detour_toward` does for singles; (c) per-member fallback: place each member via `_detour_toward`, verify `_positions_cohesive`, emit `member_dests` (the engine's `_apply_formation_move` already accepts arbitrary per-member destinations); (d) emit `ToggleAbility` candidates to switch off optional Flight/Quickness when that's the only bar to a ≥3-figure formation (Wings: 5 of 6 figures Flight-barred).
- **Measure**: archive counterfactual — formation candidates offered per turn across all 3 archives (baseline ≈ 0 after t5); self-play formation_moves/game.
- **Effort**: small→medium. **Impact**: high (action economy is the whole game at 2 actions/turn).

### 1.2 Danger/exposure term in move scoring + threat annotations (4 analysts — the most-corroborated finding)
- **What**: `_move_value` scores only distance progress and range-band entry; `evaluate_state`'s threat term exists but is **never called** by `score_candidate`. Consequences, per game: Anvil — serial suicide charges (contact concurrency never >2, each arrival dead in 1–2 turns, human dealt 40 clicks to the AI's 15); Wings — speed-10/range-10 fliers moved 28" total vs the human's 334" against a speed-6, zero-ranged horde (a guaranteed kite win), separation collapsing 17"→1.1" by t17; retreat scores a flat ~0.2 vs 4–6 for any chip attack.
- **Where**: `clixengine/ai/evaluation.py:129 _move_value` and `:203 score_candidate`; annotations in `clixengine/candidates.py` `add_move` (~L358).
- **How**: (a) annotate every move candidate with engine-computed destination facts: expected incoming clicks next turn (sum over enemies whose speed+range envelope covers dest of `hit_odds × expected_damage`), `dest_terrain` defense mod, break-away odds when engaged; (b) subtract `λ × expected_incoming_clicks × _vpc(figure)` in `_move_value`, applied also to pass/stand candidates (kills "pass in the Draconum's front arc"); (c) add a **support term** (bonus per friendly engaging the same target this turn — fixes piecemeal commitment) and a **kite candidate** for ranged figures (dest at ~own_range−0.5 away from the nearest threat, offered whenever an enemy can reach contact next turn, scored near the figure's per-turn shooting value when own speed+range > enemy speed).
- **Measure**: self-play vs the playbook bot: clicks absorbed before first attack (baseline ~20), avg end-of-turn count of AI figures inside enemy threat envelopes, kite-win conversion in a Wings-like scripted matchup.
- **Effort**: medium. **Impact**: highest of any single change — it addresses playbook pillars 1, 2, 4, and 8 simultaneously.

### 1.3 Dial-aware push cost + `push_would_demoralize` (3 analysts; a game literally ended on it)
- **What**: `_push_cost` charges a flat `_vpc` per push, but a push onto a Demoralized click removes the figure from combat AND from the victory count — strategically equal to elimination, priced at one click. Wings: 11 self-push clicks (33% of the army's dial), every one of 5 demoralizations was push-enabled, and at T42 the AI pushed its last non-demoralized figure (the 2-click Imp) into Demoralized → immediate `game_over` (seq 315→316). Humans pushed only at ≥5 remaining clicks; the AI pushed at ≤3 remaining seven times across games.
- **Where**: `clixengine/ai/evaluation.py:59 _push_cost` (+ formation variant); `clixengine/candidates.py` push annotations (~L118–126, where `push_would_eliminate` already exists); one line in `llm.py _SYSTEM`.
- **How**: compute vpc over *attack-capable* clicks only; escalate cost toward ~0.7×figure.points when `dial[click+1]` carries DEMORALIZED; annotate `push_would_demoralize: true` and `next_click_attack: n`; `_SYSTEM` states "Demoralized figures cannot attack and do not count for victory — pushing into Demoralized is losing the figure."
- **Measure**: % self-inflicted clicks in self-play (baseline 42% Wings, 5-clicks-for-1-damage Phalanx); zero game-ending self-pushes.
- **Effort**: small. **Impact**: high.

### 1.4 Support-piece targeting: Necromancy/healer awareness in scoring AND candidates (4 analysts)
- **What**: Two coupled defects. *Scoring*: `_attack_value` credits full points for kills a living necromancer refunds — Wings AI won the click war 53:3 yet removed 23 net points while losing 165 (5 of 7 kills revived at full strength; 0.43 pts/click vs the human's 9.2 — a 21× efficiency gap). *Candidates*: approach moves only target `enemies_by_dist[:3]`, so the Anvil Mending Priestess was attacked **0 times** while healing 7 clicks — more than the AI's entire net output against its declared grind target (2 clicks dealt vs 6 healed back). No prompt principle can fire when the option isn't in the menu.
- **Where**: `clixengine/ai/evaluation.py _attack_value`; `clixengine/candidates.py:~402` (approach generation).
- **How**: (a) if the target's side has a living, non-demoralized Necromancy figure, multiply elimination bonus for revivable targets by ~0.25; (b) add a support bounty: targets whose current click carries Necromancy/Healing/Magic Healing/Command score +k×points (Necromancy is click-gated — even 1 chip click on a Grave Robber disables it); (c) always generate approach/charge candidates toward enemy support figures and the highest-value shooter regardless of distance rank; (d) one `_SYSTEM` line: "Kill enablers first — kills next to a living necromancer are refunded."
- **Measure**: archive counterfactual: turn of first candidate targeting the Priestess (baseline t37) / a Grave Robber (baseline t20 attack, t36 mention); self-play net-points-per-click vs a healer/necromancy scripted army.
- **Effort**: small→medium. **Impact**: high.

### 1.5 Draft guards bundle (3 analysts; one game unwinnable at turn 0)
- **What**: (a) **Budget floor**: Phalanx fielded 111/200 pts because `server.py:422-423` breaks the loop the moment `pick()` returns None, with ~89 affordable points (incl. a same-faction healer) still in the pool — mean attack 8.0 vs mean defense faced 16.1, mathematically lost at draft. (b) **Legality validation**: Wings justified 3 of 6 picks with "ranged formation" synergy that `engine.py:1485` forbids for Mage Spawn (5/6 of its figures) — 0 formation actions all game vs the human's 24. (c) **Role checklist**: 0/3 AI drafts had a healer; the human's healer/necromancers decided both attrition games; both pools offered healers (Phalanx even same-faction). (d) **Ability-click honesty**: `_fig_brief` lists whole-dial abilities, so the drafter bought "Vampirism sustain" that lives on click 6 only (0 uses ever).
- **Where**: `clixengine/server.py` draft loop (~L422); `clixengine/build.py` `pick()` (:307), `_fig_brief`, `_affordable` (:88), heuristic top-up (:321-338, already exists — reuse).
- **How**: refuse `-1` while `remaining > max(10, 0.05×budget)` and `_affordable()` is non-empty (re-prompt once with "you still have N points", then heuristic top-up); add `formation_capable: bool` (helper already exists in build.py) to `_fig_brief` and re-prompt when a rationale cites formations for a barred figure; inject "you have no healer; these candidates heal: [...]" after 60% budget spent; annotate abilities with click locations and drop DEMORALIZED from the top-abilities list; surface `used_llm=False` fallback picks in draft_notes (the Wings 77-pt Jarl centerpiece was a silent heuristic fallback).
- **Measure**: 100 seeded drafts: % of budget spent (baseline 55.5% worst case), % armies with a healer (baseline 0), % rationales citing illegal mechanics (baseline 3/6 picks in Wings).
- **Effort**: small. **Impact**: high — removes the "lost before turn 1" games entirely.

### 1.6 Free-spin: threat-weighted facing (2 analysts; 3 of 5 Phalanx deaths)
- **What**: `server.py:293 _auto_free_spin_opponents` deterministically faces the last mover. The human farmed it with the same 2-figure bait three times: contact with a cheap figure (12-pt Nightblade) forces the spin, the expensive figure already in contact gets a guaranteed rear kill. Human rear attacks 16 vs AI 2 lifetime; Wings 11/11 human attacks rear.
- **Where**: `clixengine/server.py:293`.
- **How**: replace `angle_to(fig, mover)` with argmax over ALL adjacent enemies of `(attack × damage × hit_odds_vs_me)`; face the bisector when two threats fit in the front arc; skip the spin when it would newly expose a stronger adjacent enemy. (Longer-term: surface as a picker candidate, but the heuristic alone kills the exploit.)
- **Measure**: rear attacks suffered per game in self-play vs the pin-then-flank playbook bot (baseline: 3 of 5 deaths in Phalanx via this exact pattern).
- **Effort**: small (≈one function). **Impact**: high.

### 1.7 Terrain placement orientation fix (3 analysts, identical placements in 3/3 games)
- **What**: `terrain_placement_candidates` scans y-ascending from y=4 (the human's edge) and `_where_label` (engine.py:180) calls y<12 "near your side" for BOTH players. All 9 AI terrain pieces across 3 games landed at y=6 in the human's half; the human's Magus camped on an AI-placed hill for 9 turns collecting +1 defense while blasting.
- **Where**: `clixengine/engine.py:180 _where_label`, `:192 terrain_placement_candidates`; TerrainPlacer context payload.
- **How**: make `_where_label` owner-relative; band the candidate scan by the owner's deploy zone (own half first, then midfield, offering a spread of rows per shape); include deploy-zone coordinates in the placer payload; simple army-profile bias (melee → midfield LoF-blockers on the approach lane; ranged → own-half hills).
- **Measure**: trivially — % of AI placements in own half/midfield (baseline 0/9); self-play '(around terrain)' detour counts (baseline 6–7 per game).
- **Effort**: small. **Impact**: medium (but it's a pre-game handicap in every game).

### 1.8 Snapshot correctness + dial futures + threat facts (2 analysts)
- **What**: (a) **Bug**: `snapshot.py:62` computes `actions_remaining` as `actions_per_turn() - len(_acted_uids)` — a 5-figure formation reports −3 with an action still available (engine truth is `engine._actions_remaining()`, which exists). (b) **Blindness**: the AI sees current-click stats only while the human client renders full dials — it cannot see the Demoralized cliff, kill thresholds, or stat cliffs (it called the 2-click Imp "healthy" before the game-ending push). (c) No relational facts: `_SYSTEM` forbids the model from doing geometry, yet nothing tells it who can reach/shoot whom.
- **Where**: `clixengine/snapshot.py` `_figure_view` (L23-42), `board_snapshot` (L56-67).
- **How**: one-line `actions_remaining` fix (public alias for `_actions_remaining`); add `remaining_clicks`, `clicks_to_demoralized`, next 2–3 clicks' stats for all figures (parity with the human's `view.py _dial_view`); add an engine-computed `threats` block per AI figure (enemies that can attack it next turn, expected clicks, `at_risk_of_elimination` flag) — same machinery as 1.2's annotations.
- **Measure**: rationale audit in self-play logs (does the model cite reach/threat facts — baseline: 0 mentions of enemy range/speed numbers); paired with 1.2's positional metrics.
- **Effort**: small. **Impact**: high (multiplies the value of every prompt-layer principle).

### 1.9 Prompt corrections bundle (3 analysts; includes a factually wrong rule)
- **What**: (a) `chat.py:39-40 _RULES` states push damage arrives on the THIRD consecutive turn — the engine charges it on the SECOND and forbids a third; this contradicts the correct P4-R4 text in `_SYSTEM` twenty lines earlier and is injected into every battle/draft/chat prompt. It also conflates close-formation (2–3, touch the target) with ranged/movement (3–5, touch each other) sizes. (b) Replace "spreading actions across fresh figures beats hammering one" with "mass, then commit together — never feed figures into a ranged line one at a time" (defeat-in-detail was pillar #2; contact concurrency never exceeded 2). (c) Add: demoralized own figures are strategic corpses — never spend actions relocating them (Anvil burned ~8 endgame turns "preserving" a figure that cannot count for victory). (d) Expose the heuristic rank/score in each option with "id 0 = engine's greedy best; deviate only for a stated strategic reason", and demote doctrine flavor to background ("doctrine never justifies a negative-EV action" — it licensed both bad pushes and passive retreats).
- **Where**: `clixengine/chat.py _RULES`; `clixengine/ai/llm.py _SYSTEM` (L27-65), `_ranked_candidates` (L125-136), `_prompt` (L138-175).
- **How**: text edits + passing `heuristic_rank`/normalized score through `_prompt`.
- **Measure**: A/B in self-play with LLM picker on a small game sample; rationale audits (push-at-≤3-clicks count, actions spent on demoralized figures).
- **Effort**: small. **Impact**: medium.

## Tier 2 — Next wave

### 2.1 Rear-arc contact candidates (4 analysts)
`_contact_point` (candidates.py:72) always lands on the near side — the AI *cannot* flank, while `_SYSTEM` tells it to. Generate a second contact candidate behind the target (from `target.facing`), prioritized when a friendly already pins the target frontally (the free spin is spent — this is exactly the human's pattern), annotated `rear: true` with the improved odds. Small effort. Measure: rear-attack rate (baseline 2 vs 16).

### 2.2 Attack value: retaliation, kill-completability, and the odds floor (2 analysts)
Phalanx fed 5 of 9 attacks into the DV17-18 Toughness Draconum at 17–42% odds (2 clicks gained on a 9-click dial; the Draconum killed both attackers at 97–100%) because `_attack_value` = expected clicks × vpc with no counterattack term and no completion weighting. Add: subtract expected retaliation for close attacks (`target_odds × target_damage × _vpc(attacker)`); scale by kill-completability (divide by target remaining clicks or bonus when `my_damage ≥ remaining`); soft floor penalty below ~35% odds. **This also resolves the apparent A1-vs-A3 conflict** (see Conflicts). Medium effort. Measure: avg AI attack odds (baseline 0.43 Phalanx → target ≥0.7), net clicks per attack action.

### 2.3 Pin-the-shooter and un-base-my-shooter (2 analysts)
Basing disables shooting (P4-R23) and shields the attacker from the rest of the gunline (P4-R25); the human based the AI's entire Phalanx gunline (1 ranged attack in 21 turns) while its own shooters rolled zero break-aways in three games. Multiply charge value ~1.5× when the target has range>0 and is unengaged; annotate `pins_shooter: true`. Conversely: high-priority "break away and retreat to range" compound candidate for own based shooters, with `break_away_odds` stamped on engaged moves (engine already computes them for the human client). Medium effort.

### 2.4 Move-to-cover / take-the-hill candidates (2 analysts)
0 of 74 AI single moves ended on elevation across 3 games despite the AI placing 3 hills per game; no terrain term exists in `_move_value` and no cover guidance in the prompt. Generate nearest-hindering/hill-top destinations when under ranged threat, annotated with the defense mod; +0.3–0.6 in `_move_value`. Medium effort. Pairs with 1.7.

### 2.5 Shockwave valuation + stealth-piercing awareness (1 analyst, strong evidence)
`_shockwave_value` uses flat 0.5×vpc per foe — no hit odds, no 1-click-per-target rule, no target quality — so the AI blasted revivable zombie clusters (hitting its own 77-pt Jarl) while never aiming the one weapon that ignores Stealth at the Grave Robbers (the two GRs it *accidentally* splashed were knocked off their Necromancy clicks — proving the use). Fix the value function (per-target odds × 1 click × vpc + support bounty, friendly-fire penalty kept) and annotate `ignores_stealth_and_hindering: true` with stealthed/support figures in the blast. Small effort.

### 2.6 Turn memory + anti-thrash (2 analysts)
Each `_ask()` is stateless: the Anvil Troll Brawler oscillated between two exact points at t11/25/31/35 (0 attacks in 60 turns) as near-tied scores flipped the argmax. Include this turn's executed actions + the last 2–3 turns of rationales (already collected as `ai_notes`) in `_prompt`; have the model echo a one-line persistent plan; penalize candidates that exactly undo the figure's previous move. Medium effort.

### 2.7 Small-candidates polish batch (1 analyst each, latent or low impact)
Regeneration EV 1.0 → 1.67 (engine heals `max(0, d6-2)`); multi-target ranged combos with per-target odds and the 1-click cap stated; Necromancy revive options (the zombie/skeleton 0-click revive is never offered) and defensive Levitate pulls; pass scored slightly negative when the figure has 0 tokens. All small; batch into one PR.

## Tier 3 — Architectural bets

### 3.1 One-ply opponent-reply simulation (the PRD's own AI3/X4 — never built)
`heuristic.py` is pure greedy; PRD §9b promised shallow expectiminimax and `evaluation.py` itself documents the gap ("one-ply scoring can't yet see the follow-up… see FUT-AI lookahead"). For the top-K candidates: `apply()` on a copied engine, run the opponent's greedy best reply, re-score with `evaluate_state`, and surface the delta to the LLM ("after enemy reply: −12 material"). The engine is deterministic/copyable — plumbing, not theory. This subsumes much of Tier 1.2's hand-built danger terms and validates every "set up next turn" narrative (Phalanx narrated a pooled volley for 15 straight turns whose precondition was never once satisfiable). **Large effort; do it after Tier 1 so the eval harness can prove the payoff and the static eval it leans on is sane.**

### 3.2 Joint 2-action turn planning / mass-and-commit macro
Enumerate pairs of top-k candidates scored on the resulting state, or cheaper: a "synchronized advance" macro-candidate that holds figures at a rally point until ≥3 can reach contact the same turn. Attacks the defeat-in-detail pillar directly (every Anvil charger died solo). Large effort; 1.2's support term is the cheap 80% version.

### 3.3 AI deployment phase
All 19 deploy events across three archives are human; the AI starts in a default faction row regardless of doctrine or the terrain it just placed. One extra LLM call at deploy (or 3–4 parameterized templates: castle/line/refused-flank auto-placed). Medium effort, but only worth doing once the battle layers stop losing on their own.

### 3.4 Implement Stealth (rules gap, faithfulness + AI value)
`abilities.py` calls Stealth "inert until terrain exists" but terrain shipped; `line_of_fire` never consults it. The AI drafted and lost two Stealth Nightstalkers believing the card text the prompt feeds it. ~10 lines in `line_of_fire` + tests; also fixes faithfulness for human Stealth figures. Medium effort.

## Conflicts between analysts, resolved

1. **"Push-phobic" (analyst 5: 28 passes vs 0, longest `_SYSTEM` paragraph is push-fear) vs "over-pushes into Demoralized" (analysts 2, 6: 42% self-inflicted clicks, game-ending push).** Both are right, and both are the *same* defect: `_push_cost` is a flat `_vpc` — too expensive for a deep-dial figure with a real payoff, absurdly cheap next to a Demoralized cliff. The resolution is Tier 1.3's dial-aware cost (cheap when useful clicks abound and expected value ≥ ~1 click; near-full points at the cliff) plus scoring 0-token passes negative. Do NOT globally loosen or tighten.
2. **"Kill the weakest" bait (analyst 1: 30-pt Nightstalker died trading for a 7-pt scout — 'trade up!') vs "stop feeding the highest-vpc monster" (analyst 3: 5 of 9 attacks into the Draconum — 'prefer killable targets').** Superficially opposite target-selection advice; actually one defect: `_attack_value` ignores completion probability, retaliation, and attacker exposure. Tier 2.2's net-expected-points-swing formulation satisfies both cases: the scout kill loses because the firing position was fatal (exposure), the Draconum poking loses because 2 clicks into a 9-click Toughness dial with 97% retaliation is negative EV. Neither "always trade up" nor "always hit soft targets" should be hardcoded.
3. **Heuristic misleads the LLM (analyst 3: vpc ranking steered attacks at the Draconum) vs LLM overrides a correct heuristic (analyst 2: shockwaves scored ~0 and were taken anyway; analyst 1: doctrine flavor licensed negative-EV pushes).** Blame both, coupled by opacity: the model never sees the scores (analyst 4: `_ranked_candidates` strips them). Fix scoring (Tiers 1–2) AND expose rank/score with a "deviate only with stated reason" rule (1.9d) — either alone leaves the failure mode.
4. **Snapshot fidelity: ship real terrain polygons (analyst 4) vs ship engine-computed relational facts because `_SYSTEM` forbids geometry (analyst 6).** Resolve in favor of engine-computed facts (threat blocks, `threats_at_dest`, cover flags) — it respects the no-geometry contract and the DP2 principle; polygons are secondary and only needed if we later let the model reason spatially.
5. **Effort disagreement on formation fix (analyst 1: small; analysts 3–4: medium).** Split it: multi-target + bearing probes are small (quick-win #5); per-member fallback and ToggleAbility are the medium follow-up.

# Adversarial critique (fold these corrections into implementation)

**Verdict:** Substantially sound and unusually well-grounded: nearly every line citation checks out and the load-bearing archive statistics reproduce exactly (formation moves 1 vs 23, rear attacks 16 vs 2, passes 28 vs 0, all 9 AI terrain placements at y=6 in the human half with owner-agnostic 'near your side' labels, Phalanx 111/200 draft stopped by an LLM -1 with 89 affordable points left, Anvil Priestess attacked 0 times while healing 7, Wings' 5 zombie revives at 0 clicks and the seq-315 game-ending self-push, the snapshot actions_remaining bug, the chat.py third-turn push-rule error, Stealth never consulted in line_of_fire, and the PRD's unbuilt AI3/X4 expectiminimax promise). Tiering is broadly right: candidates + scoring + pre-game before architecture. The plan needs eleven corrections before implementation, none fatal: two mischaracterized code facts (_attack_value already has a completion bonus and ranged premium; evaluate_state's 'threat term' measures outgoing reach, not incoming danger), one non-reproducible metric family (the quoted hit-rate/attack-odds bands don't match the logs and pre-roll odds aren't in event logs at all), three feasibility caveats (straight-segment paths block most rear-contact moves; ToggleAbility works only for optional abilities, resets every turn, and costs Flight's movement perks; the Phalanx formation recovery via bearing probes is unverified), a wrong code pointer in the draft guard (pick() returns None on -1 before its fallback can run — the guard belongs in server.py), a shockwave resolution spec error (one shared roll, full damage on single target), one dropped cheap win its own 28-passes evidence supports (Pass is mislabeled — tokens clear anyway for idle figures), and a mild mis-prioritization (rear-arc candidates deserve Tier 1; the playbook bot doesn't gate the quick wins).

## Corrections

1. 2.2 and Conflicts #2 misstate the code: _attack_value does NOT lack completion weighting — evaluation.py:86-89 already adds an elimination bonus (hit_odds x target.points x 0.5 when dmg_est >= remaining+1) and :90-91 already gives ranged a no-counterattack premium (x1.05). The real gaps are: no close-combat retaliation term, no low-odds floor, binary rather than graduated completability, and no penalty for chip damage into deep dials. The Draconum feeding happened because the existing kill bonus never fires on a 9-click dial while the target's vpc is huge — the fix list is fine, but as written the patch risks double-adding a kill bonus that already exists.
